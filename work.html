Projects

Interactive Timeline: A Historic Fall at MU

I created an interactive timeline alongside another editor to contextualize the events of the tumultuous fall 2015 semester at the University of Missouri. It was cited by The New York Times, The Washington Post and other national outlets and became The Maneater's most popular piece of content ever with over 200,000 views.

Special Issue: Nov. 9, 2015

After MU's chancellor and system president resigned just hours apart following race-based student protests, our staff created a special online issue. I wrote two news stories, edited other stories and assisted in planning the issue and selecting visuals.

Interactive: Explaining Title IX

As a freshman higher education reporter, I pitched the idea for an "explainer" on Title IX to make the policies more understandable for students.

Editing

Tim Wolfe's indeterminate path to the presidency

One of my reporters worked for months to uncover the secrecy behind the search for former UM System President Tim Wolfe. After she wrote thousands of words breaking down her findings, we edited for hours to fine-tune the story and make it as clear as possible for readers.

Special Section: Two Years, No Progress

In 2014, an apartment balcony collapsed, killing a firefighter and leading to the demolition of the graduate student housing complex and child care center. I worked with two of my reporters to conceptualize a look into the university's response over the past two years.

Special Section: Work Hard, Play Loud

As The Maneater's projects editor, I worked with a writer over a period of weeks to edit this in-depth piece on Mizzou's 130-year-old marching band. I was also in charge of coordinating visuals and multimedia for the online special section.
