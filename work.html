Projects

Interactive Timeline: A Historic Fall at MU

I created an interactive timeline with another editor to contextualize the events of the tumultuous fall 2015 semester at the University of Missouri. It was cited by The New York Times, The Washington Post and other national outlets and became The Maneater's most popular piece of content ever with over 200,000 views.

Special Issue: Nov. 9, 2015

After MU's chancellor and system president resigned just hours apart following race-based student protests, our staff created a special online issue. I wrote two news stories, edited other stories and assisted in planning the issue and selecting visuals.

Interactive: Explaining Title IX

As a freshman higher education reporter, I pitched the idea for an "explainer" on Title IX to make the policies more understandable for students. I gathereed information, wrote the copy and assisted with the interactive web design.

Graphics

I've created a variety of interactive and print graphics to accompany stories. I'm interested in using data to tell stories that aren't as easily conveyed in words.

Interactive Map: Building Names

I looked into the namesakes of MU's buildings with another reporter, and we found that 77 out of 94 are named after white men. I created the interactive map below to accompany the story using Google Fusion Tables.

Click on the dots for information about each building. The colors represent gender and ethnicity. Red — white male; green — white couple; yellow — white female; blue — black male; pink — company/foundation.

Interactive: Breaking Down Clery Releases since 2011

I used Tableau to create a series of graphics categorizing all Clery releases, which are emails informing MU students and employees of crimes reported on campus, that had been sent out since 2011. I also mapped where the incidents occurred on campus using Google Fusion Tables.

Interactive: MU reaches largest total enrollment in school history

I used Tableau to visualize trends in MU's enrollment data from the past six years.

One of my reporters worked for months to uncover the secrecy behind the search for former UM System President Tim Wolfe. After she wrote thousands of words breaking down her findings, we edited for hours to fine-tune the story and make it as clear as possible for readers.
