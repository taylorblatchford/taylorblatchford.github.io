I'm Taylor, a student journalist at the University of Missouri.

I like to tell stories.

This is my story as a journalist.

High School

I fell in love with journalism in high school, starting as a reporter and working my way up to editor-in-chief my senior year. I gained experience in writing, photography, design and digital media and led my peers in producing content for a newsmagazine, website, yearbook and broadcast. In the spring of 2014, I had the honor of being named the Journalism Education Association's National High School Journalist of the Year.

The interactive map below, showing the college destinations of nearly 500 members of the senior class, is one of the projects I'm most proud of from high school.

College

I chose to attend the University of Missouri to study at one of the top journalism schools in the world. I'm planning on majoring in convergence journalism to learn how to best tell stories with multiple tools. I'm also considering a double major in secondary education because I would love to eventually be a high school journalism adviser.

The Maneater

I'm a higher education beat writer and copy editor for MU's weekly student newspaper, The Maneater. I've covered university news topics including the search for a new provost, Title IX and the university's Board of Curators. I've also contributed photos and graphic design to the publication.

Teaching

I traveled to the JEA/NSPA national high school journalism convention in Washington, D.C. to teach sessions to high school students in November 2014. I will attend the same convention in Denver in April 2015 to deliver a speech at the opening ceremony and teach more sessions.
